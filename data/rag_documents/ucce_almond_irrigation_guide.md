# UC Cooperative Extension: Almond Irrigation Management Guide
## San Joaquin Valley Edition

### Seasonal Water Requirements

Almonds in the San Joaquin Valley require approximately 42-48 inches of applied water per year,
depending on soil type, rootstock, and canopy coverage.

**Monthly ET estimates (mature orchard, full canopy):**

| Month | ETc (inches) | Critical Notes |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| March | 2.5 | Bloom — avoid deficit, supports fruit set |
| April | 4.2 | Rapid shoot growth, leaf expansion |
| May | 5.8 | Kernel fill begins — critical period starts |
| June | 7.2 | Hull split preparation — **most critical month** |
| July | 7.8 | Hull split and harvest — reduce irrigation 2 weeks pre-harvest |
| August | 6.5 | Post-harvest — maintain canopy for next year's buds |
| September | 4.0 | Reduced demand, begin tapering |

### Interpreting Satellite Vegetation Indices for Almonds

**NDVI ranges for healthy almond orchards (June-July):**
- Healthy, fully irrigated: 0.55 - 0.75
- Mild stress (monitor): 0.40 - 0.55
- Moderate stress (investigate): 0.30 - 0.40
- Severe stress (immediate action): < 0.30

**NDWI interpretation:**
- Positive NDWI (> 0.0): Adequate canopy moisture
- Slightly negative (-0.1 to 0.0): Monitor — early water stress
- Negative (< -0.1): Water stress confirmed — check soil moisture sensors

**Key distinction**: Low NDVI with positive NDWI may indicate nutrient deficiency rather than
water stress. Low NDVI with negative NDWI strongly suggests water stress.

### Deficit Irrigation Strategies

Regulated Deficit Irrigation (RDI) can be applied during specific phenological stages:
- **Post-harvest (Aug-Sep)**: 60-75% ETc is acceptable without yield impact
- **Kernel fill (May-June)**: NO deficit — full ETc required
- **Hull split (July)**: Mild deficit (85-90% ETc) may actually improve hull split uniformity

### Heat Stress in Almonds

When temperatures exceed 105°F:
- Leaf stomata close, reducing transpiration and photosynthesis
- Kernel oil quality may be affected
- Sunburn damage to exposed nuts and branches
- Increase irrigation frequency (not volume) to maintain soil moisture availability

**Satellite detection**: Heat-stressed trees may show temporarily depressed NDVI (by 0.05-0.10)
even with adequate soil moisture. This typically recovers within 5-7 days if temperatures moderate.
Cross-reference with weather data before attributing NDVI dips to water stress.

### Soil Moisture Monitoring

Recommended sensor placement:
- 12 inches depth (active root zone)
- 36 inches depth (deeper root zone, drainage monitoring)
- One station per irrigation management zone (typically 20-40 acres)

**Trigger points for irrigation (sandy loam soil):**
- Field capacity: 0.28 m³/m³
- Management Allowable Depletion (MAD): 0.18 m³/m³ (50% depletion)
- Permanent wilting point: 0.08 m³/m³

### Common Misdiagnosis

1. **Salinity stress vs. water stress**: Both reduce NDVI. Salinity stress is more uniform
   across a field and doesn't respond to increased irrigation. Check EC of irrigation water.
2. **Root disease vs. drought**: Phytophthora root rot causes localized tree decline that
   looks like water stress on satellite imagery. Field verification needed.
3. **Young trees vs. stressed trees**: Orchards < 5 years old have lower NDVI due to incomplete
   canopy coverage, not stress. Normalize by tree age.
