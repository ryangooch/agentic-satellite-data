# Central Valley Water District Advisory Bulletin
## July 2024 — Irrigation Season Update

### Current Water Supply Status

**Federal CVP Allocation**: 75% of contract amount (improved from initial 50% allocation)
**State Water Project**: 30% of requested amounts
**Groundwater**: Pumping restrictions in effect for Madera and Tulare subbasins per SGMA

### July Irrigation Advisory

Given current allocations and extreme heat forecasts, the district recommends:

1. **Priority irrigation**: Permanent crops (almonds, pistachios, grapes) should receive
   full ET replacement. Annual crops may need to be deficit irrigated.

2. **Night irrigation**: Where possible, shift irrigation to nighttime hours (10 PM - 6 AM)
   to reduce evaporative losses. Studies show 10-15% efficiency gain.

3. **Cover crop management**: Mow or incorporate cover crops in orchard middles to reduce
   competition for limited water.

### Heat Wave Advisory (July 8-22, 2024)

The National Weather Service has issued an Excessive Heat Warning for the San Joaquin Valley.
Temperatures are forecast to reach 108-115°F in interior locations.

**Agricultural impacts:**
- Almond hull split will accelerate; monitor closely
- Grape berry shrivel risk is elevated
- Livestock heat stress — ensure adequate shade and water
- Irrigation system demand will peak; check for pressure drops

**Satellite monitoring note**: Expect temporary NDVI depression across all crop types during
the heat wave period. This is a normal physiological response (stomatal closure) and does NOT
necessarily indicate irrigation failure. Compare pre- and post-heat-wave imagery rather than
interpreting mid-event images in isolation.

### Groundwater Update

- Average depth to groundwater in Madera subbasin: 142 feet (down 8 feet from July 2023)
- The Madera County GSA has announced a 10% reduction in groundwater pumping allocations
  effective August 1, 2024
- Growers relying primarily on groundwater should plan for reduced availability in August

### Canal Maintenance Schedule

- Madera Canal: Scheduled maintenance shutdown August 12-16
- Friant-Kern Canal: No planned shutdowns through September
- Chowchilla Main Canal: Brief shutdown August 3 for gate repair (estimated 24 hours)

Growers receiving water from Madera Canal should pre-irrigate by August 10 to bridge
the maintenance window.
