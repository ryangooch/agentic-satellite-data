# Madera County Agricultural Crop Report 2024

## Overview
Madera County remains one of California's top agricultural producers, with gross agricultural
value exceeding $3.2 billion in 2024. The county encompasses approximately 1,374 square miles
in the heart of the San Joaquin Valley.

## Top Crops by Value
1. **Almonds** — $982 million (30.7% of county total)
2. **Pistachios** — $621 million
3. **Grapes (wine and table)** — $412 million
4. **Milk and dairy** — $389 million
5. **Corn (silage)** — $156 million

## Water and Irrigation
- Total irrigated acreage: 285,000 acres
- Primary water sources: Madera Irrigation District, Chowchilla Water District, groundwater
- Groundwater levels continued to decline in 2024, with average drop of 2.3 feet
- The Sustainable Groundwater Management Act (SGMA) compliance deadline of 2040 is driving
  significant fallowing programs, with an estimated 15,000-20,000 acres expected to be retired

## Key Concerns for 2024
- **Heat stress**: July 2024 saw 18 days above 100°F, causing sunburn damage in almonds and
  reduced fruit set in grapes. Estimated losses: $45 million county-wide.
- **Water allocation**: Federal CVP allocation was 75% for agricultural users, up from 40% in 2023
  but still below the 100% allocation needed for full irrigation schedules.
- **Dust and PM2.5**: Fallowed fields contributed to particulate matter concerns,
  particularly during July-August wind events.

## Almond Production Notes
- Bearing acreage: 98,500 acres
- Average yield: 2,180 lbs/acre (below 5-year avg of 2,340 lbs/acre)
- Hull split timing was 7-10 days early due to accumulated heat
- Navel orangeworm pressure was moderate; harvest timing was critical
- Irrigation demand: 42-48 inches per acre depending on soil type
- Key stress periods: June hull split through July harvest preparation
- Trees on sandy loam soils showed more water stress symptoms than those on clay loam

## Soil Types
- Eastern foothill zone: sandy loam, well-drained, requires more frequent irrigation
- Central valley floor: clay loam to silty clay, better water retention
- Western zone: alkaline soils with drainage challenges

## Pest and Disease
- **Almond**: Navel orangeworm, hull rot (Rhizopus), bacterial blast (spring)
- **Grapes**: Powdery mildew, vine mealybug, Pierce's disease (limited areas)
- **General**: Grasshoppers were problematic on fallowed land borders in August
